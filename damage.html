Arms Warrior DPS Guide in Battle for Azeroth (BfA)

General Information

Welcome to Arms Warrior DPS guide for World of Warcraft Battle for Azeroth (BfA). Here, you will learn everything you need to know about playing an Arms Warrior in a raid environment and in Mythic+ dungeons.

About Author

This guide has been written by Archimtiros, the Warrior class theorycrafter, and one of the best DPS Warriors in the world, who raids in Infinity. You can follow him on Twitter and you can watch his stream on Twitch.

1. Class Overview

Arms Warriors in Battle for Azeroth are powerful juggernauts which revolve around building burst damage windows with Colossus Smash. Their greatest strength comes in the form of an incredibly versatile toolkit, allowing Arms Warriors to adapt to virtually any situation, particularly when facing multiple opponents.

2. Battle for Azeroth changes for Arms Warrior

Although the core of the rotation is functionally similar to that of Legion, several of the abilities used in it have changed.

2.1 Core Mechanical Changes

  • Avatar and Rallying Cry (formerly Commanding Shout) are now on the global cooldown. Charge incurs a shorter 0.5 second global cooldown.
  • Colossus Smash replaces Battle Cry as Arms' major cooldown, and Overpower takes Colossus Smash 's place in the Legion rotation.
  • Arms has a new Mastery, Mastery: Deep Wounds, which inflicts a six second bleed after using Mortal Strike , Execute, Cleave, and Bladestorm, effectively increasing the damage of those abilities, spread out over a few seconds. The damage from this bleed can critically strike and benefit from damage-increasing buffs and debuffs.
  • Most abilities have had their Rage cost increased, slowing the rotation's pace down from Legion. This is somewhat compensated by several talents, though the spec still plays slower as a whole.
  • Taking melee damage now generates 3 Rage per incoming attack, once per second.
  • Ability damage uses a new formula including both attack power and weapon damage, whereas previously some abilities ignored weapon damage. As a result, strength is more important, and ability damage scales more consistently.
  • Personal buffs and debuffs such as Colossus Smash no longer increase the damage of trinkets or other non-class ability damage.

2.2 Baseline Abilities

  • Battle Shout is a new ability which increases party and raid Attack Power by 10%.
  • Colossus Smash is no longer reset by Tactician or increased by Mastery, acting as a more static primary DPS cooldown.
  • Execute now costs 20-40 Rage instead of 10-40.
  • Mortal Strike is no longer reset by Tactician, making its use more methodical, and costs 30 Rage.
  • Overpower is now baseline, and buffs Mortal Strike, stacking twice, and resets via Tactician procs.
  • Sweeping Strikes is now a baseline activated ability, causing all single target abilities to hit a second target for full damage, and apply effects such as Colossus Smash, Rend, and Deep Wounds to both targets.

3. Arms Warrior's Strengths

  • Bursty, cooldown driven damage.
  • Versatile toolkit, able to effectively deal with 2 targets by using Sweeping Strikes, 3-5 targets with Cleave, and burst AoE with Bladestorm.
  • Customizable rotation due to numerous talents which replace or enhance existing abilities.

4. Arms Warrior's Weaknesses

  • Some reliance on Tactician to fill global cooldowns.
  • Slow rotation, with intermittent periods of unfilled global cooldowns.
  • Lack of sustained combat healing.

5. Viability of Arms Warrior in the Current Patch

Due to the influence of legendaries and tier bonuses that the specialization is no longer designed for, individual performance can vary greatly. In high-end gear, Arms is out-performed by Fury on single-target, although it has better two-target and burst AoE tools.




Fury Warrior DPS Guide in Battle for Azeroth (BfA)

General Information

Welcome to Fury Warrior DPS guide for World of Warcraft Battle for Azeroth (BfA) 8.0.1. Here, you will learn everything you need to know about playing a Fury Warrior in a raid environment and in Mythic+ dungeons..

About Author

This guide has been written by Archimtiros, the Warrior class theorycrafter, and one of the best DPS Warriors in the world, who raids in Infinity. You can follow him on Twitter and you can watch his stream on Twitch.

1. Class Overview

Fury Warriors in Battle for Azeroth have recieved many improvements from Legion, particularly to talents, improving multitarget capabilities, and Execute. The amount of stackable cooldowns has been reduced, streamlining the rotation to make Rampage and Enrage the most important gameplay elements, while built-in Haste buffs make Fury one the fastest action specializations in the game. In additon to a well-rounded offensive toolkit, Fury boasts high mobility, strong self healing, and one of the few raid-wide defensive cooldowns in the game.

2. Battle for Azeroth changes for Arms Warrior

Although the core of the rotation remains similar to Legion, several improvements have been made to talents, multitarget, and the Execute phase.

2.1 Core Mechanical Changes

  • Recklessness (formerly Battle Cry) and Rallying Cry (formerly Commanding Shout) are now on the global cooldown. Charge incurs a shorter 0.5s global cooldown.
  • Fury no longer takes increased damage while Enraged, but also no longer has a significantly higher health pool than other classes, although it does still have slightly higher stamina due to dualwielding two-handed weapons.
  • Taking melee damage now generates 3 Rage per incoming attack, once per second.
  • Rage generation from autoattacks and abilities has been increased significantly to account for the loss of legendaries and artifact traits.
  • Ability damage uses a new formula including both attack power and weapon damage, whereas previously some abilities ignored weapon damage. As a result, strength is more important, and ability damage scales more consistently.
  • Personal buffs and debuffs such as Enrage no longer increase the damage of trinkets or other non-class ability damage.

3. Furry Warrior's Strengths

  • Innate Haste buffs allow for a fast and spammy play style.
  • Deals multitarget damage without sacrificing single target damage.
  • Strong burst AoE abilities which are also useful on single target.
  • Short priority rotation that is easy to learn.

2.2 Baseline Abilities

  • Battle Shout is a new ability which increases party and raid Attack Power by 10%.
  • Bladestorm now deals its damage quicker, at the expense of less ticks.
  • Bloodthirst has a flat 30% chance to trigger Enrage, rather than based on critical strike.
  • Enrage increases Haste by 25%, instead of autoattack speed.
  • Execute generates 20 Rage on a 6-second cooldown, reduced by Haste.
  • Raging Blow has two charges, a 20% chance to reset its own cooldown on use, and no longer requires Enrage.
  • Rampage animation and global cooldown is reduced by Haste.
  • Recklessness has become a resource cooldown, doubling Rage generation and increasing Critical Strike chance by 20% for 10 seconds.
  • Victory Rush has been added back to Fury.
  • Whirlwind now generates Rage and replaces Furious Slash in the base rotation. Its buff now causes the next two single-target abilities to cleave, including Execute.

4. Furry Warrior's Weaknesses

  • The Haste buff from Enrage creates feast or famine style gameplay, which may be unappealing to some.
  • Ramp up time is needed in order to generate Rage for Rampage, made more difficult when not enraged.
  • While there is some amount of customization, talents do not significantly change the core rotation.

5. Viability of Furry Warrior in the Current Patch

Due to the influence of legendaries and tier bonuses that the specialization is no longer designed for, individual performance can vary greatly. In high end gear, Fury should out perform Arms on single target, although it has slightly lower burst AoE.
