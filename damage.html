Arms Warrior DPS Guide in Battle for Azeroth (BfA)

General Information

Welcome to Arms Warrior DPS guide for World of Warcraft Battle for Azeroth (BfA). Here, you will learn everything you need to know about playing an Arms Warrior in a raid environment and in Mythic+ dungeons.

About Author

This guide has been written by Archimtiros, the Warrior class theorycrafter, and one of the best DPS Warriors in the world, who raids in Infinity. You can follow him on Twitter and you can watch his stream on Twitch.

1. Class Overview

Arms Warriors in Battle for Azeroth are powerful juggernauts which revolve around building burst damage windows with Colossus Smash. Their greatest strength comes in the form of an incredibly versatile toolkit, allowing Arms Warriors to adapt to virtually any situation, particularly when facing multiple opponents.

2. Battle for Azeroth changes for Arms Warrior

Although the core of the rotation is functionally similar to that of Legion, several of the abilities used in it have changed.

2.1 Core Mechanical Changes

  • Avatar and Rallying Cry (formerly Commanding Shout) are now on the global cooldown. Charge incurs a shorter 0.5 second global cooldown.
  • Colossus Smash replaces Battle Cry as Arms' major cooldown, and Overpower takes Colossus Smash 's place in the Legion rotation.
  • Arms has a new Mastery, Mastery: Deep Wounds, which inflicts a six second bleed after using Mortal Strike , Execute, Cleave, and Bladestorm, effectively increasing the damage of those abilities, spread out over a few seconds. The damage from this bleed can critically strike and benefit from damage-increasing buffs and debuffs.
  • Most abilities have had their Rage cost increased, slowing the rotation's pace down from Legion. This is somewhat compensated by several talents, though the spec still plays slower as a whole.
  • Taking melee damage now generates 3 Rage per incoming attack, once per second.
  • Ability damage uses a new formula including both attack power and weapon damage, whereas previously some abilities ignored weapon damage. As a result, strength is more important, and ability damage scales more consistently.
  • Personal buffs and debuffs such as Colossus Smash no longer increase the damage of trinkets or other non-class ability damage.

2.2 Baseline Abilities

  • Battle Shout is a new ability which increases party and raid Attack Power by 10%.
  • Colossus Smash is no longer reset by Tactician or increased by Mastery, acting as a more static primary DPS cooldown.
  • Execute now costs 20-40 Rage instead of 10-40.
  • Mortal Strike is no longer reset by Tactician, making its use more methodical, and costs 30 Rage.
  • Overpower is now baseline, and buffs Mortal Strike, stacking twice, and resets via Tactician procs.
  • Sweeping Strikes is now a baseline activated ability, causing all single target abilities to hit a second target for full damage, and apply effects such as Colossus Smash, Rend, and Deep Wounds to both targets.

3. Arms Warrior's Strengths

  • Bursty, cooldown driven damage.
  • Versatile toolkit, able to effectively deal with 2 targets by using Sweeping Strikes, 3-5 targets with Cleave, and burst AoE with Bladestorm.
  • Customizable rotation due to numerous talents which replace or enhance existing abilities.

4. Arms Warrior's Weaknesses

  • Some reliance on Tactician to fill global cooldowns.
  • Slow rotation, with intermittent periods of unfilled global cooldowns.
  • Lack of sustained combat healing.

5. Viability of Arms Warrior in the Current Patch

Due to the influence of legendaries and tier bonuses that the specialization is no longer designed for, individual performance can vary greatly. In high-end gear, Arms is out-performed by Fury on single-target, although it has better two-target and burst AoE tools.




Fury Warrior DPS Guide in Battle for Azeroth (BfA)

General Information

Welcome to Fury Warrior DPS guide for World of Warcraft Battle for Azeroth (BfA) 8.0.1. Here, you will learn everything you need to know about playing a Fury Warrior in a raid environment and in Mythic+ dungeons..

About Author

This guide has been written by Archimtiros, the Warrior class theorycrafter, and one of the best DPS Warriors in the world, who raids in Infinity. You can follow him on Twitter and you can watch his stream on Twitch.

1. Class Overview

Fury Warriors in Battle for Azeroth have recieved many improvements from Legion, particularly to talents, improving multitarget capabilities, and Execute. The amount of stackable cooldowns has been reduced, streamlining the rotation to make Rampage and Enrage the most important gameplay elements, while built-in Haste buffs make Fury one the fastest action specializations in the game. In additon to a well-rounded offensive toolkit, Fury boasts high mobility, strong self healing, and one of the few raid-wide defensive cooldowns in the game.

2. Battle for Azeroth changes for Arms Warrior

Although the core of the rotation remains similar to Legion, several improvements have been made to talents, multitarget, and the Execute phase.

2.1 Core Mechanical Changes

  • Recklessness (formerly Battle Cry) and Rallying Cry (formerly Commanding Shout) are now on the global cooldown. Charge incurs a shorter 0.5s global cooldown.
  • Fury no longer takes increased damage while Enraged, but also no longer has a significantly higher health pool than other classes, although it does still have slightly higher stamina due to dualwielding two-handed weapons.
  • Taking melee damage now generates 3 Rage per incoming attack, once per second.
  • Rage generation from autoattacks and abilities has been increased significantly to account for the loss of legendaries and artifact traits.
  • Ability damage uses a new formula including both attack power and weapon damage, whereas previously some abilities ignored weapon damage. As a result, strength is more important, and ability damage scales more consistently.
  • Personal buffs and debuffs such as Enrage no longer increase the damage of trinkets or other non-class ability damage.

3. Furry Warrior's Strengths

  • Innate Haste buffs allow for a fast and spammy play style.
  • Deals multitarget damage without sacrificing single target damage.
  • Strong burst AoE abilities which are also useful on single target.
  • Short priority rotation that is easy to learn.

2.2 Baseline Abilities

  • Battle Shout is a new ability which increases party and raid Attack Power by 10%.
  • Bladestorm now deals its damage quicker, at the expense of less ticks.
  • Bloodthirst has a flat 30% chance to trigger Enrage, rather than based on critical strike.
  • Enrage increases Haste by 25%, instead of autoattack speed.
  • Execute generates 20 Rage on a 6-second cooldown, reduced by Haste.
  • Raging Blow has two charges, a 20% chance to reset its own cooldown on use, and no longer requires Enrage.
  • Rampage animation and global cooldown is reduced by Haste.
  • Recklessness has become a resource cooldown, doubling Rage generation and increasing Critical Strike chance by 20% for 10 seconds.
  • Victory Rush has been added back to Fury.
  • Whirlwind now generates Rage and replaces Furious Slash in the base rotation. Its buff now causes the next two single-target abilities to cleave, including Execute.

4. Furry Warrior's Weaknesses

  • The Haste buff from Enrage creates feast or famine style gameplay, which may be unappealing to some.
  • Ramp up time is needed in order to generate Rage for Rampage, made more difficult when not enraged.
  • While there is some amount of customization, talents do not significantly change the core rotation.

5. Viability of Furry Warrior in the Current Patch

Due to the influence of legendaries and tier bonuses that the specialization is no longer designed for, individual performance can vary greatly. In high end gear, Fury should out perform Arms on single target, although it has slightly lower burst AoE.

Welcome to Frost Death Knight DPS guide for World of Warcraft Battle for Azeroth (BfA) . Here, you will learn everything you need to know about playing a Frost Death Knight in a raid environment and in Mythic+ dungeons..

About Author

This guide has been written by Abyssalwave, a top level Death Knight player. He is a former US #2 hardcore raider who currently raids with Club Camel. You can also check out his YouTube channel.

1. Class Overview

The Frost specialisation provides reasonable single target and AoE DPS and it seems to be on par with Unholy in terms of performance. Its playstyle is straightforward and its rotation is easy to learn.

2. Battle for Azeroth changes for Frost Death Knight

  • Many notable changes to the entire talent tree.
  • Death's Advance has replaced Wraith Walk (which is now a talent) as the baseline movement ability.
  • Pillar of Frost is now a 45-second cooldown that increases your Strength by 15% for 15 seconds. Each Rune you spend while Pillar of Frost is active increases your Strength by an additional 1% for the rest of the duration.
  • Empower Rune Weapon now works the same as Hungering Rune Weapon did in Legion. It increases your Haste by 15% and generates 1 Rune and 5 Runic Power instantly and every 5 seconds for 20 seconds on a 2-minute cooldown (down from 3 minutes in Legion).

3. Frost Death Knight Strengths

  • Easy spec to learn
  • Good sustained cleave damage
  • Very strong short burst AoE damage

4. Frost Death Knight Weaknesses

  • Lacks mobility, making it one of the slowest specs in the game
  • Requires many small decisions at crucial moments in order to maximise the DPS rotation

Enhancement Shaman DPS Guide in Battle for Azeroth (BfA)

General Information

Welcome to Enhancement Shaman DPS guide for World of Warcraft Battle for Azeroth (BfA) . Here, you will learn everything you need to know about playing an Enhancement Shaman in a raid environment and in Mythic+ dungeons.

About Author

This guide has been written by Wordup, a frequent theorycrafter involved in a number of class communities. He is also an experienced player who has been in the world top 100 since the days of Sunwell, currently raiding in Echoes. You can also follow him on Twitter.

1. Class Overview

Enhancement Shaman has remained largely intact in Battle for Azeroth, with the core abilities carrying over a lot of the same design from Legion. It still keeps a focus on single target tools with some easy access into additional sustained cleave and AoE without it altering the rotation significantly. Losing the artifact has diluted some parts of what made Enhancement strong within the Legion landscape, however its ability to maintain a strong sustained damage package should not be underestimated. It can feel quite susceptible to damage however with a lack of strong defensive tools, so requires a good amount of awareness to stay safe. It also still keeps the large package of flexible small utility tools that are useful in a variety of situations, but largely remains as a specialization that does not provide anything truly unique.

2. Battle for Azeroth changes for Enhancement Shaman

The core of Enhancement remains, however some mechanical changes have been made alongside some new abilities being added.

2.1 Existing Ability Changes

  • The Maelstrom cap has been reduced to 100, down from 150.
  • Stormstrike has been reduced to a 30 Maelstrom cost, and had its base cooldown reduced to 9 seconds.
  • Stormbringer now grants a free Stormstrike cast, instead of reducing the cost by 50%.
  • Stormlash has been removed from the Enhancement toolkit.






3. Enhancement Shaman's Strengths

  • Strong sustained single target damage.
  • Flexible, can mesh with a number of different encounter styles.
  • Able to funnel AoE and cleave into single target, gaining a large amount of priority target damage.
  • Potential to do extremely high burst damage to targets with RNG at your side.
  • Versatile group utility toolkit for both dungeons and raids.

2.2 New Spell and Mechanic Additions

  • Lava Lash now costs 40 Maelstrom and deals a larger amount of damage as a Maelstrom spender.
  • Lightning Surge Totem returns as a baseline spell, and has been renamed to Capacitor Totem, with an increased cooldown of 1 minute and a reduced stun duration of 3 seconds.
  • Earth Elemental returns to the spec, and is the same as the Elemental Shaman equivalent.
  • Tremor Totem returns to the specialization as a Fear, Charm and Sleep removal tool.
  • The artifact trait Raging Storms has been rolled into Stormbringer as a baseline effect.
  • The artifact trait Gathering Storms has been rolled into Crash Lightning as a baseline effect.

4. Enchancement Shaman's Weaknesses

  • High variance in burst due to Stormbringer.
  • Reliant on uptime, so can be punished in very high movement encounters.
  • Talent tree lacks diversity, so not a lot of personal customization can be done on an encounter specific basis.
  • Weak defensives, so cannot take a lot of punishment.



Feral Druid DPS Guide in Battle for Azeroth (BfA)

General Information

Welcome to Feral Druid DPS guide for World of Warcraft Battle for Azeroth (BfA) 8.0.1. Here, you will learn everything you need to know about playing a Feral Druid in a raid environment and in Mythic+ dungeons.

About Author

This guide has been written by Wordup, a frequent theorycrafter involved in a number of class communities. He is also an experienced player who has been in the world top 100 since the days of Sunwell, currently raiding in Echoes. You can also follow him on Twitter.

1. Class Overview

Feral, the Melee DPS specialisation for Druids, forms its core gameplay around balancing two separate resources whilst also leveraging those to apply powerful Bleed Damage over Time effects on targets. In terms of single target DPS, Feral is powerful and can often shine in these situations. When it comes to multiple target situations, Feral can perform well due to increased targets to apply Bleed effects too, and has large-scale AoE tools to contribute well with.

2. BFA Changes for Feral Druid

Feral remains largely intact, with very few changes being implemented aside from some systems alterations with how some stats and mechanics operate.

  • Berserk now only reduces Energy costs by 40%, and is now on the Global Cooldown.
  • Tiger's Fury now lasts 10 seconds and only grants 50 Energy.
  • Your Bleed effects ( Rake, Rip, and Thrash) now deal damage faster based on your Haste.
  • Dash has been reduced to a 2-minute cooldown, and is down to a 60% movement speed bonus for 10 seconds.
  • Bear Form has been reduced to a 25% Stamina gain, but the Armour increase has raised to 220%.
  • Hibernate and Soothe return as baseline spells.

3. Feral Druid's Strengths

  • Solid single target damage
  • Very high mobility and flexibility in a raid environment
  • Powerful burst AoE option in Brutal Slash
  • Strong low target cleave achieved through maintaining DoTs on multiple targets
  • Flexible talent choices allowing for advantages in different fight archetypes

4. Feral Druid's Weaknesses

  • Can require ramp-up to reach full damage on priority targets
  • Gameplay relies on pooling and smart resource management, and can be punishing
  • Can be hampered by enemies dying too early before your Bleeds deal their full damage
  • Weak burst cooldown options even considering talent choices



Havoc Demon Hunter DPS Guide in Battle for Azeroth (BfA)

General Information

Welcome to Havoc Demon Hunter DPS guide for World of Warcraft Battle for Azeroth (BfA) . Here, you will learn everything you need to know about playing a Havoc Demon Hunter in a raid environment and in Mythic+ dungeons.

About Author

This guide has been written by Wordup, a frequent theorycrafter involved in a number of class communities. He is also an experienced player who has been in the world top 100 since the days of Sunwell, currently raiding in Echoes. You can also follow him on Twitter.

1. Class Overview

Havoc in Battle for Azeroth has gone through somewhat of a maturing process, trying to focus more on the talent tree and allowing for more diverse options in gameplay approaches, alongside tightening up some of the more loose aspects of the original implementation. It still remains a predominantly builder spender style rotation and is largely Global Cooldown locked. The core hook is making the most of your Fury pool and reacting to unpredictable gains, ensuring you use high burst cooldowns and windows to push yourself even further. On top of that, it comes with a variety of mobility and defense tools, creating a versatile toolkit with some unique abilities that can be used in a variety of ways.

2.1. Existing Ability Changes

  • The Fury cap has been raised from 100 to 120.
  • Chaos Strike now has a fixed 40% change to refund 20 Fury, instead of being fueled by Critical Strikes.
  • Eye Beam has been reduced to a 30 Fury cost baseline, its cooldown has dropped to 30 seconds, and deals 50% additional damage to your current target.
  • Chaos Nova now only stuns for 2 seconds, but has a 30% chance to spawn a Soul Fragment per stunned target baseline.
  • The Havoc interrupt has been renamed to Disrupt, which has a 15-second cooldown, 3-second lockout, but reduced to a 10-yard range. It also only generates 30 Fury now on a successful interrupt.

2.2. New Spell and Mechanic Additions

  • Consume Magic is an offensive dispel that removes 1 beneficial Magic effect from your target with a 20-yard range and 10-second cooldown. Successfully removing an effect also generates 20 Fury.
  • Torment is a 3-second duration taunt with a 30-yard range and 8-second cooldown.
  • Demonic Wards reduces all Magic damage taken passively by 10% at all times.
  • Chaos Brand causes enemies hit by your Chaos damage to take an additional 5% Magic damage from all sources with an unlimited duration.

3. Havoc Demon Hunter's Strengths

  • Powerful burst single target damage
  • Extremely powerful cooldowns for high damage requirement moments
  • Flexible mobility allowing for great positioning control in encounters
  • Can switch targets without any downsides, and can pool resources for instant burst
  • Powerful, versatile cooldowns for different situations
  • Strong group utility, especially via Chaos Brand

4. Havoc Demon Hunter's Weaknesses

  • AoE is tied to cooldowns, which hurts sustained damage
  • Some RNG is built into the resource system, which can on rare occasions lead to starvation at bad moments
  • Outside of cooldown windows, damage can be lackluster
  • Some talents have problems with certain encounter types, which can hinder options.

5. Viability of Havoc Demon Hunter in the Current Patch

Due to the expansion having a lot of changes over the course of testing, and some of those being extremely recent it is difficult to gauge where it will land. The specialization comes primed with a lot of different tools and a talent tree robust enough to allow for flexibility, alongside powerful defensive and mobility tools. That said, it remains to be seen as to how far this will carry. All things point toward Havoc being a very powerful melee option currently, however.




Retribution Paladin DPS Guide in Battle for Azeroth (BfA)

General Information

Welcome to Retribution Paladin DPS guide for World of Warcraft Battle for Azeroth (BfA) . Here, you will learn everything you need to know about playing a Retribution Paladin in a raid environment and in Mythic+ dungeons.

About Author

This guide has been written by Urthearso, theorycrafter for Retribution Paladins. If you have any questions about the spec you can reach him on the official Paladin Discord, on Twitter, and during his streams on Twitch.

1. Class Overview

In single-target DPS, the Retribution specialisation is currently performing very well, compared to most other classes. The sustained AoE performance on the other side is rather weak.

2. BFA Changes for Retribution Paladins

  • Mastery: Hand of Light increases all Holy damage done by our abilities. Note that this does not affect Racials and Trinket effects .
  • Avenging Wrath and Shield of Vengeance are now affected by the global cooldown.
  • Avenging Wrath now grants 20% increased damage, healing, and Critical Strike chance.
  • Judgment now generates 1 Holy Power and causes the target to take 25% increased damage from your next Holy Power spender.
  • Art of War is a new passive, giving us the Legion version of Blade of Wrath with half proc rate at 2 procs per minute.
  • Shield of Vengeance's absorb shield now equals 30% of your maximum Health. It will burst when the shield is consumed, or after 15 seconds, and deal Holy damage equal to the absorbed amount divided to nearby enemies. The absorb shield and damage do not get increased by any damage modifiers.
  • Retribution's duration has been halved to 10 seconds.
  • Crusader Strike's cooldown has been increased to 6 seconds, up from 4.5.

3. Strengths of Retribution Paladin

  • Strong single-target sustained damage
  • Good single-target burst damage
  • Can switch targets without downsides
  • Many utility spells such as Greater Blessing of Wisdom, Blessing of Protection, and Blessing of Freedom
  • Strong defensives like Divine Shield, Eye for an Eye, and Shield of Vengeance

4. Weaknesses of Retribution Paladin

  • Low sustained AoE damage
  • Low mobility
  • Uptime-reliant



Assasination, Outlaw and Subtlety Rogue DPS Guide in Battle for Azeroth (BfA)

Assassination Rogue

About Author

This guide has been reviewed and approved by Gray_Hound, a Rogue theorycrafter who raids in Strawberry Puppy Kisses. You can find him on Twitch and Twitter.

1. Class Overview

Assassination Rogues deal damage through the application and empowerment of poison and bleed effects. The damage toolkit of Assassination is somewhat limited, mainly focusing on dealing single target damage over a long period. As with the other Rogue specializations, Assassination sports phenomenal defensive abilities that allow them to soak and survive mechanics that most other classes cannot.

2. BFA Changes for Assassination Rogue

Overall, very little changed for Assassination. Its core gameplay remains the same going into Battle for Azeroth; a number of abilities that were lost in the Legion prune have returned as talents along with some artifact effects, however the core gameplay is unchanged.

  • Bleed and Poison damage over time effects scale with Haste.
  • Feint has a cooldown, and cannot be maintained permanently.
  • Mastery: Potent Assassin also affects bleed damage.

3. Assassination Rogue Strengths

  • Strong and consistent single target damage
  • Strong sustained cleave (good for council fights)
  • Exceptional defensive utility

4. Assassination Rogue Weaknesses

  • Burst AoE and short-term cleave
  • Very cooldown-reliant, and cooldowns only affect one target

5. Assassination Rogue Viability in the Current Patch

Assassination is currently fairly competitive with both of the other Rogue specializations. It is hard to say definitively which spec will be the best for Uldir, as there are still numerous number changes going out, and all three specializations have the mechanical potential to be the best, so this will come down mostly to tuning. That being said, Subtlety does appear to sim about 2% higher than Assassination.

Outlaw Rogue

About Our Author

This guide has been reviewed and approved by Gray_Hound, a Rogue theorycrafter who raids in Strawberry Puppy Kisses. You can find him on Twitch and Twitter.

1. Class Overview

Ahoy! Outlaw Rogue is a specialization that centers around managing resources while sustaining high damage to stacked targets. As in previous expansions, Outlaw is a fantastic jack of all trades that can comfortably handle any situation or fight type. Between the three Rogue specializations, Outlaw is the most effective in situations involving heavy cleave or AoE.

2. BFA Changes for Outlaw Rogue

Overall, Outlaw has not received significant changes. The single target rotation remains effectively the same as before, with minor deviations depending on talents. The only change that is noticeable is that Blade Flurry cannot be kept up 100% of the time, and as such, on prolonged AoE fights, you will need to work on timing it well, to line it up with your other cooldowns or maximum number of targets, to min-max damage. However, on short-term burst AoE , especially if it is infrequent, that still might not be too noticeable; due to having multiple charges, you can chain back to back.

  • Blade Flurry is no longer a toggle, and cannot be maintained permanently.
  • Feint has a cooldown, and cannot be maintained permanently.
  • Grappling Hook is now baseline.

3. Outlaw Rogue Strengths

  • Strong cleave and AoE
  • Very versatile
  • Exceptional defensive utility

4. Outlaw Rogue Weaknesses

  • Variance in damage output
  • Weak in spread AoE targets can be quickly switched to, but not in cleave range
  • Resource/cooldown-reliant
  • Average single target damage

5. Outlaw Rogue Viability in the Current Patch

Outlaw is currently fairly competitive with both of the other Rogue specializations. It is hard to say definitively which spec will be the best for Uldir, as there are still numerous number changes going out, and all three specializations have the mechanical potential to be the best, so this will come down mostly to tuning. That being said, Subtlety does appear to sim about 2% higher than Outlaw. However, Outlaw does appear to have an edge over other specializations where it comes to AoE burst in lower level keystones.

Subtlety Rogue

About Our Author

This guide has been written by Ashine, European Champion of the second Mythic Dungeon Invitational and a member of two top 60 guilds, Wiping as Intended and Group Therapy. He is regarded as one of the top Subtlety Rogues in the world and contributes a fair bit to the spec's theorycrafting. You can watch him play on Twitch.

1. Class Overview

Subtlety has not received significant changes in Battle for Azeroth. Subtlety Rogues continue to provide strong single-target pressure, but it now brings respectable AoE to the table as well. Much of the rotation involves actively shifting between two stances, in and out of Stealth. Some abilities can be used at anytime, however, certain abilities can only be used while you are in Stealth. There are only a limited number of ways to activate Stealth during combat, and therefore the core component of this specialization revolves around managing your abilities to correctly time windows of burst.

2. BFA Changes for Subtlety Rogue

  • With the pre-patch, Artifact traits and the Artifact ability Goremaw's Bite have been removed.
  • Backstab and Shadowstrike now slow your target by 30%.
  • Nightblade no longer slows your target. Instead, it reduces the effectiveness of healing on the target by 15%.
  • Feint now has a 15-second cooldown and reduces damage taken from area-of-effect attacks by 40% (was 50%).
  • Cloak of Shadows now has 2-minute cooldown (was 1.5 minutes).
  • Shadow Blades is partially redesigned. Now causes your Combo Point generating abilities to deal 50% additional damage as Shadow instead of causing auto attacks to deal Shadow damage. In addition to this, Shadow Blades is now on global cooldown in Battle for Azeroth.
  • Shuriken Storm deals 50% increased damage when Shadow Dance is active (was 100%).
  • Shadow Dance has 5 second base duration (was 4 seconds).
  • Shuriken Combo increases damage of your next Eviscerate by 8% per stack, stacking up to 5 times (was 10% per stack).
  • Weaponmaster causes your Shadowstrike and Backstab to have a 15% chance to hit the target twice each time they deal damage. In Legion it gave a 6% chance to every ability to deal damage twice.
  • Master of Subtlety has been removed and is replaced by Find Weakness in the Level 15 Talent row.
  • Anticipation has been removed and is replaced by Marked for Death in the Level 45 Talent row.
  • Shot in the Dark is a new Level 75 Talent.
  • Night Terrors is a new Level 75 Talent.
  • Dark Shadow increases your damage done while Shadow Dance is active by 25% (was 30%).
  • Alacrity gives your finishing moves a 20% chance per Combo Point spent to grant 2% Haste for 20 seconds stacking up to 5 times (was 2% per stack and stacked up to 10 times).
  • Death from Above is now a PvP only talent and was replaced by Secret Technique in the Level 100 Talent row.
  • Shuriken Tornado is a new Level 100 Talent.

3. Subtlety Rogue Strengths

  • Cleave fuels additional single-target damage
  • Versatile, can specialize into multiple things by changing talents
  • Phenomenal defensive capabilities

4. Subtlety Rogue Weaknesses

  • Requires cerebral management of Combo Points and Energy to be effective
  • Low AoE damage without Secret Technique

5. Subtlety Rogue Viability in the Current Patch

Due to how versatile Subtlety Rogue is in Battle for Azeroth, Subtlety is looking to be one of the strongest specializations for raiding and dungeons. Subtlety is able to provide immense single-target in raids while having an option to spec into more AoE damage for dungeons with Secret Technique .

1. Class Overview

Survival is the Melee Hunter spec. It has a versatile toolkit with lots of Crowd Control potential, very high burst AoE damage potential and good single-target damage.

2. BFA Changes for Survival Hunter

  • Before anything else, it is worth noting that Survival is still a melee spec and there is no way to do competitive damage purely from range. Survival has a cooldown, Aspect of the Eagle, that allows its main melee ability, Raptor Strike, to be used from a 40-yard range for 15 seconds.
  • Focus now regenerates passively at a much slower rate.
  • Many abilities that were not on the global cooldown before are now affected by it, including main cooldowns like Coordinated Assault.
  • All pet specs now do the same damage, but the pet specs now provide different bonuses for defense and utility.
